City-wide strategy to ensure timely and appropriate response in case of potential shocks to the electricity supply network.

Eletricity Supply - Contingency Plan

Considerations

All sources of electricity supply should be included in the assessment (e.g. city network, generators, solar panels, windmills, etc.).

A contingency plan usually contains the definition of roles and responsibilities, communication strategy, technical procedures, and, ideally, associated budgeting. It is often multi-sectoral, but particularities regarding the electricity network should be explicit.

Take into account the relevance of regional and national level contingency plans in the local context and select the most appropriate government level in which the plan was developed.

Examples

If the plan has been elaborated, drills have been conducted, and defined roles and responsibilities have been assigned and informed, it is considered as 'implemented'.

If the only available document is a national plan, but the relevant local actors have not been informed and responsibilities assigned, it should be considered as 'developed but not implemented', and the 'national' level should be selected.
