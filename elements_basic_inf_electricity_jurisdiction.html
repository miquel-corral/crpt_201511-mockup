Local Government Jurisdiction   
Level of authority of the local government over the electricity supply network decision-making, taking into account strategical, tactical and/or operational aspects.

Eletricity Supply - Local Government Jurisdiction

Considerations

All sources of electricity supply should be included in the assessment (e.g. city network, generators, solar panels, windmills, etc.).

This entails all relevant local government bodies (such as infrastructure planning, policy-making, system operation), and the government competences to perform the management of the service and the coordination of the different stakeholders involved.

The different levels of jurisdiction of the local government over the different components of the system should be considered separately.

Examples

A city with a local public enterprise and a private supplier providing electricity can have different levels of jurisdiction over each one. For the former, it would normally 'fully controls and operates' over the entity, whilst for the latter it could exert influence over the decision-making process, however no involvement in the operation.
